\prob{2.29 d}  Noting that $\I{X, Y; Z} = \I{Z; X, Y}$ can be written as

\begin{align}
\I{X, Y; Z} &= \I{Z; X, Y} \notag \\
\I{X; Z \mid Y} + \I{Z; Y} &= \I{Z; Y \mid X} + \I{X; Z}   \notag \\
\I{X; Z \mid Y} &= \I{Z; Y \mid X} + \I{X; Z} -  \I{Z; Y} \notag 
\end{align}

thereby yeilding the desired result.
