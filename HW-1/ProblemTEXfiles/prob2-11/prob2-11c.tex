\prob{2.11 c}  Recalling the result in \ref{c2p11eq2}, it is clear that the only way for $\rho = 0$ to hold, is for $\I{X_1; X_2} = 0$ to also hold.  Since $\I{X_1; X_2} = 0$ only holds for independent $X_1$ and $X_2$, $\rho = 0$ can hold iff $X_1$ and $X_2$ are independent.
